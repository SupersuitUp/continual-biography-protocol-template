# How my parents met

<!-- Example capture. Delete once real content exists. -->

**Q: Where does the story begin?**

It begins before me, honestly. My mother sold eggs at the Saturday market and my father was the one customer who kept coming back without a basket. He bought one egg at a time so he would have a reason to return the next week. She knew exactly what he was doing. She let him do it for two months.

**Q: What was the town like then?**

A river town. Everybody worked the mill or fed the people who did. We were poor the way a field is poor in winter: everything still there, just waiting.

**Q: You said "the winter with the grandparents" earlier. What happened?**

The house fire. I was seven or eight. We lost the kitchen wall and the roof over it, and the four of us kids went to my grandparents' farm until spring. That winter is where half my good memories live, which feels wrong to say about a fire.
